Working in Economics, Statistics and Information Management

Government is transforming the way it delivers services to the public and business, and digital is at the heart of this revolution.

Join the revolution

It all started with GOV.UK, which has changed the way government speaks to people – and set new expectations from the public on how they want to access services.

Digital is how we deliver our services, but behind the technology is something far more important – an obsession with users: finding out what they need, and then delivering it to them in the simplest, clearest and fastest way possible. We help all government departments attract, develop and retain the people and skills they need to achieve the transformation they’re looking for.

The Digital Data & Technology (DDAT) profession is there to embed digital skills within every department. We’re smart and passionate about our work, and we have big plans. We believe in developing best practice and then sharing it throughout government. You’ll be encouraged to develop your skills: our in-house GDS Academy delivers a huge range of training and development – not just in digital and data, but in project management, user experience (UX) and user research. And we’re happy for you to try them all - whatever your formal job role.

Be at the centre of change

We work across government. You'll be based in a particular department, but you can move around - applying your skills to areas as diverse as tax, pet passports and improving the census.

You'll also get the chance to work on huge, cross-department project like our creation of a Global Digital Marketplace, or supporting the development of smart cities Where else will you get these sort of opportunities?

Are we right for each other?

Our people are collaborators and communicators. When you work at DDAT, we’ll expect you to contribute your skills wherever they’re needed. You’ll fit in here if you’re self-motivated and a self-starter: if you need to grab some colleagues for a sprint’s worth of experimentation (yes, we’re Agile) then we’ll support you.

Whatever your technical skills, you’ll need to share our obsessive focus on users. And we’ll support your development - with industry-leading training and a work culture where front-end developers can sit in on UX sessions, content specialists can work alongside service designers.

A lot of us work at GDS in London, but you’ll find roles in every department: there are pretty big DDAT teams in HMRC, Defra and the Department for International Trade.
